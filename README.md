## ðŸ§­ Guiding Principles: A Post-Corporate, Purpose-Driven Model

This document outlines a speculative model for governance that shifts societal incentives from profit-seeking to purpose-driven contribution.

It assumes a technological future where automation handles execution, freeing humans for the work of direction, design, and collaboration.

### 1. Core Economic & Governance Structure

* **Federated City-States (Polis):** Replaces the nation-state. Governance is localized. City-states manage common resources for their citizens.
* **Competitive Governance:** City-states compete for citizens. Mobility is a citizen's primary check on power. A city must provide a high-quality UBI, good services, and a desirable culture to thrive.
* **State-Stewarded Resources:** All non-personal property (land, natural resources, automated factories) is publicly owned by the city-state.
* **The "Rent" -> UBI Engine:** Citizens "rent" these resources (e.g., land for a home, factory time for a project). This rent is the city's sole income, which is redistributed to all citizens as a Universal Basic Income (UBI). This UBI untethers survival from "work."

### 2. The Information Commons

* **All Information is Open Source:** All intellectual property is abolished. Patents, copyrights, and trade secrets do not exist.
* **Compounding Innovation:** Every new discovery, algorithm, or artwork is immediately available to all of humanity, becoming the new baseline for everyone. This eliminates redundant, competitive R&D and enables rapid, collaborative problem-solving.
* **No Artificial Barriers:** Planned obsolescence and other market-driven inefficiencies are eliminated, as the incentive to create them (profit) is gone.

### 3. The New "Work" & Organization

* **The End of the Corporation:** The legal structure of the for-profit corporation is obsolete.
* **Purpose-Driven Organization:** Humans still organize to achieve goals, but use different models:
    * **Collectives:** Temporary groups form for a specific project (e.g., "Build a new bridge"). They petition the city-state for resources (renting the automated builders).
    * **Cooperatives:** Groups run local services (e.g., a bakery) as a democratic co-op, earning supplemental income on top of their UBI.
    * **Guilds/Associations:** Non-profit bodies that set standards, manage training, and advocate for resources (e.g., a "Biotech Guild").
* **The Human Role:** As automation handles *execution* (building, manufacturing, logistics), human work becomes:
    * **Direction & Organization:** Deciding *what* to do next (e.g., "Let's cure this disease," "Let's explore that moon").
    * **Design & Curation:** The "why" and "how" (e.g., "Build the bridge *here*," "Design it to be beautiful," "Decorate this public space to be inspiring").
    * **Inquiry:** Pure research, philosophy, and art.

### 4. The Goal: An Economy as a Utility

The economy ceases to be the *purpose* of society and instead becomes a *utility* that serves society. The goal is not to "serve the economy" but to enable humanity to solve problems, create, and flourish.
